Therapeutic areas​

Delay in diagnosis costs time, money and worsens outcomes and quality of life for patients. Improving disease recognition even for rarely encountered conditions in primary care can help us to find and help patients faster.

Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy Protocol

A tool developed to identify patients at risk of DMD based on the screening recommendations in current literature. Three pathways to diagnosis were identified and a tool built which guides the clinician through a series of steps to determine risk and work towards diagnosis.

hATTR Protocol

A pilot tool in development screening patients of Black African/ Afro-Caribbean ethnicity over 65 years for a hereditary form of transthyretin-medicated amyloidosis. A multi-system disease, hATTR may vary in presentation between individuals, meaning step by step support for physicians may help to highlight at-risk patients.

Cushing’s syndrome protocol

Input of two specific symptoms by the GP triggers a protocol to assess risk of Cushing’s in the patient, with alternative pathways based on risk stratification, and recommends appropriate testing and referral, generating documentation for primary care

Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy

1,2 Affects ~1/3500 male births worldwide with no UK-wide screening programme. Mean age of DMD diagnosis in the UK is 53.9 months (almost 5 years). Rapid diagnosis is important as DMD is progressive and life threatening.

Hereditary transthyretin-mediated (hATTR) amyloidosis3,4

A rare disease with an estimated 50,000 patients worldwide, hATTR has a median survival of 4.7 years from diagnosis. Diagnostic delay severely impairs quality of life, and hATTR often presents symptoms which mimic other neuropathic conditions. Symptoms may appear from age 30.

Cushing’s Syndrome5,6

A rare endocrine disorder, Cushing’s syndrome caused by abnormal amounts of cortisol, with a number of causes. The prevalence of Cushing’s is low; an estimated 10-15 per million each year in the US. However, a higher prevalence has been found in people with other conditions and it may be underdiagnosed.
