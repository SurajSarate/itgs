Clinical decision making is most effective when it incorporates physician expertise, patient characteristics and evidence-based practice.

To best support collaborative care, the process of integrating what is known about a suspected diagnosis with individual patient factors may be improved with algorithmic tools

​Primary care services have the greatest potential to reduce health inequalities, as the first point of contact for patients1
​General practitioners may not have the time or specialist knowledge to rapidly diagnose conditions in a single consultation

Each tool is a disease-specific algorithm, devised to improve the efficiency of diagnosis, reduce variation in practice and speed up the pathway from patient consultation to testing to diagnostic confirmation.

How algorithmic screening optimises care

Precision risk assessment

Clinician input is integrated with the patient’s digital profile and checked against risk factors to improve quality and safety in diagnosis

Improved efficacy

Accurate and swift diagnosis has both cost benefit and benefit to the patient population

Best practice

Reduces variation in clinical practice by incorporating guidelines and expert disease understanding

Reduces time from consultation to diagnosis

Allows rapid assessment, investigative testing, and referral for diagnosis

Collaborative approach​

Each tool is developed through an extensive research and planning process, helping early adopters of innovation in primary care to address unmet need in their community

​Current best practice and research is reviewed to establish the optimum treatment pathway, collaborating with expert clinicians and drawing on their diagnostic experience
